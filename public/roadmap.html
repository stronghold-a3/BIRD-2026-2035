Vision 2035: A Hub for Resilient and Ethical Growth

By 2035, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region will be recognized as the Southeast Asia's premier halal, agro-industrial, and sustainable investment destination—a self-sustaining, export-linked economy delivering inclusive prosperity anchored in Islamic values, cultural identity, and moral governance.

Vision Framework: The strategic vision integrates five core pillars—Halal Economy Dominance, Green Economy Monetization, Infrastructure Excellence, BIMP-EAGA Integration, and Islamic Finance Ecosystem—creating a synergistic development model that leverages BARMM's unique cultural authenticity, strategic location, and natural resource endowments.

Balanced Scorecard Strategic Perspectives

Four interconnected perspectives translating strategic vision into actionable objectives, measurable outcomes, and accountable implementation across all BEIE clusters

Strategic Objective F1: Scale Investment Approvals

Target: ₱15B Annual Investment Approvals by 2035

From ₱5.1B baseline (Q1 2026), implementing investment promotion roadshows, BIMP-EAGA missions, and enhanced aftercare services to attract quality domestic and foreign investments aligned with halal, agro-industrial, and green economy priorities.

Strategic Objective F2: Expand Export Revenue

Target: ₱40B+ Annual Export Value

From <₱10B baseline, establishing UAE/GCC halal export corridors, BIMP-EAGA trade facilitation mechanisms, and export readiness programs to position BARMM products in premium international markets.

Strategic Objective F3: Mobilize Islamic Finance

Target: ₱20B+ Islamic Banking Assets

From <₱2B baseline, expanding Al-Amanah and CARD Islamic Bank networks, deploying fintech solutions, and developing takaful products to channel Shariah-compliant capital across MSMEs, agro-entrepreneurs, and green enterprises.

Strategic Objective F4: Activate Green Economy Revenue

Target: ₱500M+ Annual Carbon/PES Revenue

From zero baseline, operationalizing REDD+ programs, Payment for Ecosystem Services frameworks, and eco-tourism user fees to monetize BARMM's environmental stewardship and forest endowments.

Strategic Objective S1: Enhance Investor Confidence

Target: 85% Investment Project Retention Rate

Establishing problem resolution mechanisms, expanding one-stop shop services, and ensuring predictable policy environment to maintain investor satisfaction at 8.0+/10 and prevent capital flight after incentive periods expire.

Strategic Objective S2: Empower MSMEs through Halal Certification

Target: 5,000+ Halal-Certified MSMEs

From ~500 baseline, implementing BHB MSME certification programs, BHIDP 2025-2030 rollout, and QA lab deployment to enable small enterprises to access premium halal markets and global value chains.

Strategic Objective S3: Reduce Poverty through Inclusive Investment

Target: <20% Poverty Incidence

From 34.8% (2023), implementing jobs-focused investment facilitation, MSME development programs, and inclusive value chains to ensure economic growth translates into broad-based prosperity and human development.

Strategic Objective S4: Improve Financial Inclusion

Target: 25% Adult Financial Inclusion

Expanding Islamic banking branches to all provincial capitals, deploying mobile banking solutions for archipelagic communities, and establishing waqf-managed community enterprises to channel capital to underserved populations.

Strategic Objective P1: Streamline Investment Facilitation

Target: 1-Day Digital Business Registration

From multiple days processing, implementing digital BNR system, BEGMP e-governance platform, and one-stop shop expansion to reduce transaction costs and improve ease of doing business rankings.

Strategic Objective P2: Strengthen Halal Certification Infrastructure

Target: Full OIC/SMIIC Accreditation + MRA

From pending status, building BHB capacity, signing international Mutual Recognition Agreements, and establishing QA laboratories to reduce certification time from 45-60 days to 21-day international benchmark.

Strategic Objective P3: Accelerate Infrastructure Delivery

Target: >90% Budget Execution Rate

From <80% baseline, enhancing MPW-BBOI-LGU coordination, resolving ROW issues, implementing PPP frameworks, and deploying trigger-based budgeting to ensure critical infrastructure keeps pace with investment growth.

Strategic Objective P4: Synchronize BMOA Programs

Target: 8/10 Inter-Agency Coordination Score

From ~5.5/10 baseline, institutionalizing Bangsamoro Investment Command Center, establishing inter-agency MOAs, and deploying digital coordination platforms to prevent fragmented delivery and "Tragedy of the Commons" dynamics.

Strategic Objective L1: Build Halal Expertise

Target: 100+ Certified Halal Officers

From <50 baseline, establishing Halal Training Academy, BHB-SMIIC partnerships, and international secondments to build technical capacity in certification, auditing, and quality assurance systems.

Strategic Objective L2: Develop Islamic Finance Professionals

Target: 50+ Certified Islamic Finance Experts

From <20 baseline, implementing BSP-aligned scholarship programs, professional certification pathways, and IsDB training partnerships to build expertise in Shariah-compliant banking, takaful, and sukuk structuring.

Strategic Objective L3: Strengthen IPA Capacity

Target: 80% IPA Staff Certified/Trained

From <50% baseline, implementing capacity building programs, BBOI-MTIT HRD plans, and performance incentives to professionalize investment promotion and aftercare services across all provincial facilitation offices.

Strategic Objective L4: Improve Functional Literacy

Target: 75%+ Functional Literacy Rate

From 59.3% baseline (lowest nationally), deploying TESDA programs, DepEd alternative learning systems, and adult literacy campaigns to resolve the binding human capital constraint on industrial investment viability.

Four Strategic Options Analysis

Comprehensive evaluation of alternative development pathways through multi-criteria assessment examining economic impact, feasibility, identity alignment, systems leverage, risk-return profile, inclusivity, and sustainability

Strategic Options Formulation: Through rigorous TOWS matrix analysis and systems archetype mapping, four distinct pathways emerged: (1) Halal Economy Dominance Strategy (HEDS) scoring 7.61/10, (2) Green Economy Monetization Strategy (GEMS) at 7.16/10, (3) Infrastructure-First Enabling Strategy (IFES) at 7.48/10, and (4) Integrated Ecosystem Development Strategy (IEDS) achieving the highest composite score of 8.93/10 with perfect 10.0/10 systems leverage rating. The IEDS was recommended as it uniquely addresses all five systems archetypes while synchronizing cluster development to achieve the ₱550B GRDP target.

Phased Implementation Roadmap 2026-2035

Three-phase execution strategy with budget-calibrated interventions, sequenced to match infrastructure readiness, institutional capacity, and market development

01

Phase 1: Foundation Building

2026–2028 | Budget: ₱35–45 Billion

Establishing the moral governance operating system and deploying foundational enablers that unlock downstream investment viability. This phase prioritizes coherent sequencing logic by ensuring regulatory architecture, digital facilitation, and critical infrastructure are in place before scaling production or export commitments.

Key Deliverables:
  • BHB operationalized with OIC/SMIIC-aligned processes & digital traceability pilot
  • ZBIP construction milestones reached; 80% household electrification
  • Bangsamoro Forestry Code enacted; JMC No. 2026-01 signed & LGU capacity building initiated
  • 1-day digital business registration launched via BEGMP platform
  • Farm-to-market road priorities funded & ROW mechanisms streamlined
  • Bangsamoro Investment Command Center established with real-time tracking
02

Phase 2: Acceleration

2029–2032 | Budget: ₱50–65 Billion

Leveraging governance and infrastructure foundations to activate Foundations-Transformers Integration and Financiers-Ecosystem Alignment. With certification systems, energy connectivity, and logistics networks operational, focus shifts to scaling value addition and channeling capital across the ecosystem.

Key Deliverables:
  • Bangsamoro Halal Park operational (Matanog); BHB achieves international recognition
  • REDD+ program registered; first carbon credit & PES sales executed
  • Islamic banking branches in all provincial capitals; takaful & green sukuk launched
  • 5,000+ MSMEs halal-certified; 1M annual tourist arrivals; Lake Lanao eco-tourism open
  • Cold chain & warehousing hubs operational in priority provinces
  • REDD+ registered; ₱100M/year carbon/PES revenue activated
03

Phase 3: Consolidation & Global Integration

2033–2035 | Budget: ₱35–50 Billion

Consolidating BARMM's position as a self-sustaining, export-linked, and inclusive economic hub. All strategic pillars converge to achieve Foundations-Transformers-Connectors maturity and global market recognition with innovation-driven continuous improvement.

Key Deliverables:
  • OIC endorsement secured; SMIIC Mutual Recognition Agreement (MRA) signed
  • ₱500M+ annual carbon/PES revenue; eco-tourism reaches 3M visitors
  • GRDP ₱550B+; poverty <20%; ₱15B+ annual investment approvals sustained
  • BARMM recognized as ASEAN halal hub; cross-border BIMP-EAGA certification harmonized
  • Innovation hubs & halal R&D centers operational; green bonds market mature
  • 100% electrification & 85% broadband connectivity achieved
